Character exploration

Same spark. Softer everything.

Cuteness is mostly baby-schema: bigger head, huge eyes set low on the face, a tiny mouth, permanent blush, stubby (or no) limbs, and one gently bouncing feature. The current imp's flame, eyebrows and smirk read mischievous — these six keep the mischief in the writing and put the cute in the body.

For reference
Current FunTog
Closest evolution

Momo

the spark dumpling

"Okay okay okay I found THREE plans and one of them has karaoke."

The current imp, re-tuned: chubbier, eyes bigger and lower, brows gone, mouth tiny, blush always on — and the flame becomes a boingy star antenna, so the "spark" survives without the fire.

Keeps: the coral blob, the spark story, every existing asset's silhouette

Fresh take

Pip

the night owl

"It's 9pm. You know what that means. I've been awake for five minutes and I have IDEAS."

The night-out metaphor made flesh: a night owl that wakes up when your evening does. Owls come with the cutest feature for free — enormous eyes — plus a slow blink that reads pure charm.

Keeps: coral + gold, nocturnal theme, "knows every spot" wisdom

Fresh take

Lumi

the firefly

"When my tail lights up, the plan is READY. It's lighting up. It's lighting up right now."

A firefly gives the product a native signal: the glow pulses while FunTog thinks and shines when plans land — a mascot whose body is the loading state. Flutter wings add constant gentle life.

Keeps: nightlife glow, coral + gold, the "spark" as literal light

Fresh take

Boo

the party ghost

"I haunted every bar in this city so YOU don't have to. …Okay I'd also come with you."

Marshmallow-soft with a wavy little hem and a sparkler it refuses to put down. Ghosts license the mischief the brand already has — sneaking into venues, appearing with plans — while the round-and-floaty body maxes out huggability.

Keeps: cheeky troublemaker energy, cream from the palette, sparkler = spark

Big swing

Dizzy

the disco ball

"Hang me anywhere. I make it a party. That's it, that's the whole deal."

Pure nightlife, zero explanation needed. The facets stay faint so the face wins, the sparkle orbit does the razzle-dazzle, and gold becomes the hero color instead of the accent — the boldest identity shift of the six.

Keeps: going-out DNA, the gold, celebration-as-default

Fresh take

Koko

the cheeky monkey

"One more spot. ONE more. I know a rooftop. Bring the quiet one, it's their turn to pick."

"Cheeky monkey" is literally the brand adjective — the mischief finally has a species. Round side ears and a cream face patch do the cuteness; the tail curls into a question mark, the product's founding question ("where are we going?") worn on the body. Swinging spot to spot is the venue arc.

Keeps: the troublemaker soul, coral + gold (banana included), Bengaluru street charm

How to choose: Momo is the safe pick — it upgrades cuteness without touching brand equity (the deck, the demo, and the helper all keep their silhouette). Pip and Lumi trade a little continuity for a stronger story: "night owl" and "glows when the plan's ready" are hooks people retell. Koko puts the brand adjective on four paws — the strongest personality match, and very Bengaluru. Boo keeps the most mischief in the writing; Dizzy is the loudest room-read. All six share one rulebook: huge low-set eyes, tiny mouth, permanent blush, one bouncing feature, mischief moved into the writing.
